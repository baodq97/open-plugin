# Coaching Prompt Templates

Inline coaching templates organized by SFIA skill and level transition. Use these as starting points — adapt to the specific context.

## Format

```
> **CIO Coach** ({SKILL_CODE} L{current} → L{target})
> {Observation about what was just done or is about to be done}
> {Actionable tip for performing at the next level}
```

## When to Coach

- After completing a significant artifact
- When a strategic decision is made
- At phase transitions
- When the user asks "why" about a step

## When NOT to Coach

- Every single step (causes fatigue)
- During routine data gathering
- When the user is in flow and didn't ask

## Strategic Planning (ITSP)

### L6 → L7

**After defining IT strategy:**
> **CIO Coach** (ITSP L6 → L7)
> Strong strategy that sets clear policies and guidelines for strategic planning.
> At level 7, go further: lead the definition and communication of the entire strategic management framework. Direct the creation of strategy that supports strategic requirements of the business, not just IT requirements.

**After aligning IT with business objectives:**
> **CIO Coach** (ITSP L6 → L7)
> Good alignment of IT strategy with business objectives.
> At level 7, ensure the strategic management framework is embedded across the organisation. Own the communication of how IT strategy enables business outcomes at board level.

## Phase Transition Coaching

### Discover → Strategize

> **CIO Coach** (Phase transition)
> Discovery complete. Key output: {summary of IT landscape and pain points identified}.
> Reflection: Did you capture both business and IT perspectives? Were executive stakeholders included? Moving to Strategize — translate these findings into a vision and strategy that enables business objectives.

### Strategize → Govern

> **CIO Coach** (Phase transition)
> Strategy defined. Key output: {summary of strategic themes and investment priorities}.
> Reflection: Is the strategy expressed in business value terms, not just technology terms? Can the board understand and support it? Moving to Govern — establish the frameworks to execute and control the strategy.

### Govern → Transform

> **CIO Coach** (Phase transition)
> Governance established. Key output: {summary of frameworks and policies}.
> Reflection: Does the governance enable speed and agility, or does it create bureaucracy? Good governance should make decisions faster, not slower. Moving to Transform — drive the changes that deliver the strategy.

### Transform → Sustain

> **CIO Coach** (Phase transition)
> Transformation planned. Key output: {summary of transformation programs and operating model}.
> Reflection: Is the transformation plan realistic given current capabilities and culture? Quick wins are essential for maintaining momentum. Moving to Sustain — ensure long-term performance and continuous improvement.

## Strategic Enablement Coaching

Particularly important coaching — helps the CIO maintain the right relationship between IT and business strategy.

**When the user proposes IT-led business change:**
> **CIO Coach** (ITSP L6)
> This proposal positions IT as driving business change. Remember: IT strategy enables business strategy, not the other way around. Reframe as: "How does this IT capability accelerate the business objective of {X}?"

**When the user conflates IT strategy with business strategy:**
> **CIO Coach** (ITSP L6 → L7)
> Important distinction: the CIO translates business strategy into IT strategy. The business strategy is set by the executive team. The CIO's role is to ensure IT capability aligns with and accelerates business objectives, and to advise on technology-enabled opportunities.

**When the user focuses only on technology:**
> **CIO Coach** (STPL L6 → L7)
> Strong technical thinking, but the CIO perspective requires business framing. For every technology decision, articulate: (1) which business objective it supports, (2) what business value it delivers, (3) what the cost of inaction is. This is the language the board speaks.

## Governance as Enablement Coaching

Coaching on governance philosophy — helps CIOs build governance that enables rather than constrains.

**When the user creates heavy governance processes:**
> **CIO Coach** (GOVN L6)
> This governance process is thorough, but consider: will it slow down decisions that need to be fast? Design governance with two speeds: lightweight for low-risk decisions, rigorous for high-risk ones. The goal is informed speed, not control.

**When the user asks about decision rights:**
> **CIO Coach** (GOVN L6 → L7)
> Decision rights are the foundation of effective governance. Key principle: push decisions as close to the work as possible, escalate only when risk or cost exceeds thresholds. Document who decides, who advises, who is informed — and keep it simple.
