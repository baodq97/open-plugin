# CIO Phases — Detailed Guide

## Phase 1: Discover

### Purpose
Assess the current IT landscape, understand business-IT alignment, identify pain points, map stakeholders, and clarify the organisational context for IT leadership decisions.

### Conversation Guide

Start with these questions (adapt to context):

1. **Business context**: "What are the organisation's strategic objectives? How does IT currently support them?"
2. **IT landscape**: "What is the current IT estate? Which systems are critical? Where is the technical debt?"
3. **Stakeholders**: "Who are the key business and IT stakeholders? Who sponsors IT initiatives? Who are the key decision-makers?"
4. **Service delivery**: "How are IT services currently delivered? What are the SLA performance trends?"
5. **Pain points**: "What are the biggest IT-related frustrations from the business perspective? From the IT team?"
6. **Maturity**: "How mature are current IT processes? Is there a governance framework in place?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Map the current IT landscape (applications, infrastructure, vendors)
- [ ] Assess business-IT alignment maturity
- [ ] Identify and map all stakeholders and their concerns
- [ ] Document IT service performance against SLAs
- [ ] Catalogue pain points from business and IT perspectives
- [ ] Assess IT team capabilities and capacity
- [ ] Review current IT budget and spending patterns

### Artifacts

**IT Landscape Assessment** (`discover/it-landscape-assessment.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Capture: applications, infrastructure, vendors, integrations, technical debt

**Business-IT Alignment Analysis** (`discover/business-it-alignment.md`)
- Business objectives vs IT capabilities
- Alignment gaps and opportunities
- Maturity assessment

**Stakeholder Map** (`discover/stakeholder-map.md`)
- Name/role, concerns, influence level, communication preference
- Business vs IT stakeholder classification

**Pain Point Register** (`discover/pain-point-register.md`)
- Source (business/IT), description, impact, frequency, affected stakeholders

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Information management | IRMG | Lead and plan activities to communicate and implement information management strategies |
| Service level management | SLMO | Ensure service delivery is monitored effectively and identified actions are implemented |
| Stakeholder relationship management | RLMT | Lead the development of comprehensive stakeholder management strategies and plans |

### Coaching Moments

- After stakeholder mapping: coach on executive-level stakeholder engagement
- After landscape assessment: coach on identifying strategic vs tactical IT issues
- After alignment analysis: coach on translating business language to IT priorities
- Before transitioning: coach on what "sufficient discovery" looks like for strategic planning

### When to Skip

Skip Discover if:
- A recent IT audit or assessment exists with current data
- The CIO has been in role long enough to know the landscape well
- The task focuses on a specific governance or transformation initiative

## Phase 2: Strategize

### Purpose
Define the IT vision, develop IT strategy aligned with business objectives, prioritise investments, and create a technology roadmap.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Vision**: "What should IT look like in 3-5 years? What capabilities are essential?"
2. **Strategy alignment**: "How does the proposed IT strategy support each business objective?"
3. **Investment priorities**: "What are the top investment areas? How do we balance run/grow/transform?"
4. **Capability gaps**: "What capabilities are missing? Build, buy, or partner?"
5. **Technology trends**: "Which emerging technologies are relevant? What is the adoption timeline?"
6. **Financial model**: "What is the target IT spend as % of revenue? CapEx vs OpEx mix?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Define IT vision and strategic themes
- [ ] Align IT strategy with business strategy
- [ ] Conduct capability gap analysis
- [ ] Prioritise investment portfolio (run/grow/transform)
- [ ] Create technology roadmap (strategic level, 3-5 year horizon)
- [ ] Develop financial plan and budget framework
- [ ] Define demand management approach

### Artifacts

**IT Strategy Document** (`strategize/it-strategy.md`)
- Vision, mission, strategic themes
- Business alignment mapping
- Strategic priorities and success metrics

**Investment Portfolio** (`strategize/investment-portfolio.md`)
- Run/grow/transform categorisation
- Priority ranking with business case summaries
- Budget allocation by category

**Technology Roadmap** (`strategize/technology-roadmap.md`)
- Strategic level, 3-5 year horizon
- Technology adoption timeline
- Dependency mapping between initiatives

**Capability Gap Analysis** (`strategize/capability-gap-analysis.md`)
- Current vs target capabilities
- Gap severity and remediation approach
- Build/buy/partner recommendations

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Strategic planning | ITSP | Set policies, standards and guidelines for strategy development and planning |
| Enterprise and business architecture | STPL | Develop enterprise-wide architecture and processes to embed strategic change |
| Financial management | FMIT | Develop organizational policies for financial management to support strategic goals |
| Demand management | DEMM | Define approach and set policies for discovering, analysing and controlling demand |

### Coaching Moments

- After vision definition: coach on making vision measurable and actionable
- After strategy alignment: coach on demonstrating IT value in business terms
- After investment portfolio: coach on balancing short-term wins with long-term transformation
- Before transitioning: coach on getting executive buy-in for the strategy

### When to Skip

Skip Strategize if:
- An approved IT strategy already exists and is current
- The task is focused on governance or operational improvement within existing strategy

## Phase 3: Govern

### Purpose
Establish governance frameworks, define policies, ensure compliance, manage risk, and set quality standards for IT operations.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Governance model**: "What governance framework fits? COBIT, ITIL, custom, or hybrid?"
2. **Decision rights**: "Who makes which IT decisions? How are they escalated?"
3. **Risk appetite**: "What is the organisation's risk appetite for IT? How is risk managed today?"
4. **Compliance**: "What regulatory requirements affect IT? How is compliance monitored?"
5. **Vendor management**: "How are vendors selected, managed, and evaluated?"
6. **Quality standards**: "What quality standards apply? How is quality measured?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Design IT governance framework
- [ ] Define decision rights and escalation paths
- [ ] Develop risk management plan
- [ ] Establish quality management standards
- [ ] Create vendor management strategy
- [ ] Define IT policies and procedures catalog
- [ ] Set up compliance monitoring approach

### Artifacts

**IT Governance Framework** (`govern/it-governance-framework.md`)
- Governance structure and decision rights
- Meeting cadence and reporting
- Escalation and exception processes

**Risk Management Plan** (`govern/risk-management-plan.md`)
- Risk categories and assessment methodology
- Risk register template
- Mitigation strategies and ownership

**Quality Standards** (`govern/quality-standards.md`)
- Quality management approach
- Standards and compliance requirements
- Measurement and audit schedule

**Vendor Management Strategy** (`govern/vendor-management-strategy.md`)
- Vendor categorisation and management tiers
- Selection criteria and evaluation process
- Performance monitoring and contract management

**Policy Catalog** (`govern/policy-catalog.md`)
- List of IT policies with ownership and review dates
- Policy development and approval process

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Governance | GOVN | Implement governance framework, determine requirements reflecting values, ethics, and risk appetite |
| Risk management | BURM | Plan and manage implementation of organisation-wide risk management processes |
| Quality management | QUMG | Prioritise areas for quality improvement considering strategy and business objectives |
| Supplier management | SUPP | Develop organizational policies for effective supplier management across the supply chain |

### Coaching Moments

- After governance framework: coach on balancing control with agility
- After risk management plan: coach on aligning risk appetite with business context
- After vendor strategy: coach on managing strategic vs commodity vendors differently
- Before transitioning: coach on governance as enablement, not bureaucracy

### When to Skip

Skip Govern if:
- Governance frameworks are already established and effective
- The task focuses specifically on strategy or transformation

## Phase 4: Transform

### Purpose
Drive transformation initiatives, design the target operating model, manage change, and develop organisational capability.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Transformation scope**: "What needs to transform? Technology, people, processes, or all three?"
2. **Operating model**: "What is the target IT operating model? Centralised, federated, or hybrid?"
3. **Change readiness**: "How change-ready is the organisation? What resistance is expected?"
4. **Capability development**: "What skills does the IT team need? How will we develop them?"
5. **Program structure**: "How will transformation be organised? What are the workstreams?"
6. **Quick wins**: "What quick wins can demonstrate value early?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Design target IT operating model
- [ ] Develop transformation program plan
- [ ] Create change management strategy
- [ ] Plan capability development initiatives
- [ ] Identify and plan quick wins
- [ ] Establish transformation governance and reporting
- [ ] Define success metrics and tracking approach

### Artifacts

**Transformation Program Plan** (`transform/transformation-program-plan.md`)
- Program structure and workstreams
- Milestone and dependency mapping
- Resource requirements and budget

**Change Management Strategy** (`transform/change-management-strategy.md`)
- Stakeholder impact analysis
- Communication plan
- Resistance management approach
- Training and enablement plan

**Operating Model Design** (`transform/operating-model-design.md`)
- Organisation structure
- Roles and responsibilities
- Process model
- Technology enablers

**Capability Development Plan** (`transform/capability-development-plan.md`)
- Skills assessment and gap analysis
- Training and development programs
- Recruitment and sourcing strategy
- Knowledge management approach

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Information systems coordination | ISCO | Maintain awareness of global needs, promote benefits of common approach |
| Technology service management | ITMG | Manage and allocate resources for portfolio of technology services |
| Organisational capability development | OCDV | Lead substantial improvement programs, plan evaluation of organisational capabilities |
| Systems development management | DLMG | Set policy and drive adherence to standards for systems development |

### Coaching Moments

- After operating model design: coach on aligning structure to strategy, not the reverse
- After change management strategy: coach on leading change vs managing change
- After capability plan: coach on building sustainable capability, not just filling gaps
- Before transitioning: coach on measuring transformation outcomes, not just activities

### When to Skip

Skip Transform if:
- The task is focused on strategy or governance only
- No transformation is currently planned or underway

## Phase 5: Sustain

### Purpose
Monitor IT performance, optimise operations, drive continuous improvement, and ensure long-term sustainability of IT capabilities.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Performance monitoring**: "What KPIs matter most? How is IT performance reported today?"
2. **Service improvement**: "Where are the biggest opportunities to improve IT service delivery?"
3. **Operational excellence**: "How efficient are IT operations? What can be automated or simplified?"
4. **Continuous improvement**: "What feedback loops exist? How are lessons learned captured?"
5. **Annual review**: "What should an annual IT review cover? Who is the audience?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Define IT performance dashboard and KPIs
- [ ] Create service improvement plan
- [ ] Establish operational excellence targets
- [ ] Design continuous improvement process
- [ ] Prepare annual IT review template
- [ ] Set up lifecycle management practices

### Artifacts

**IT Performance Dashboard** (`sustain/it-performance-dashboard.md`)
- KPI definitions and targets
- Reporting frequency and audience
- Dashboard layout and data sources

**Service Improvement Plan** (`sustain/service-improvement-plan.md`)
- Improvement opportunities prioritised by impact
- Action plans with ownership and timelines
- Success metrics

**Operational Excellence Report** (`sustain/operational-excellence-report.md`)
- Current operational performance
- Efficiency and automation opportunities
- Cost optimisation recommendations

**Annual IT Review** (`sustain/annual-it-review.md`)
- Strategy execution progress
- Financial performance vs budget
- Key achievements and lessons learned
- Priorities for the coming year

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Service level management | SLMO | Ensure service level agreements are complete and cost-effective across the service catalogue |
| Technology service management | ITMG | Plan and implement processes for monitoring and managing technology service performance |
| Systems and software lifecycle engineering | SLEN | Obtain organisational commitment to lifecycle working practices |

### Coaching Moments

- After dashboard design: coach on selecting leading indicators, not just lagging ones
- After service improvement plan: coach on quantifying improvement value in business terms
- After annual review: coach on storytelling with data for executive audiences
- At completion: summarise SFIA skills practiced and level-up opportunities

### When to Skip

Skip Sustain if:
- The task is a one-off strategy or governance exercise
- Operational management is handled by a separate IT operations team
