# SFIA 9 Skill Map — BA-Relevant Skills

Detailed descriptions for BA-relevant SFIA skills at each level. Use this reference to understand what each level requires and to coach level transitions.

---

## Primary Skills

### Business situation analysis (BUSA)

Investigating business situations to define recommendations for improvement action.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Investigates straightforward business situations to identify and analyze problems and opportunities. Contributes to the recommendation of improvements. Follows agreed standards and techniques to investigate, analyze and document business situations. Engages with stakeholders under direction.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Investigates business situations where there is some complexity and ambiguity. Adopts holistic view to identify and analyze problems and opportunities. Contributes to the selection of the approach and techniques to be used. Conducts root cause analysis and identifies recommendations for improvements. Engages and collaborates with operational stakeholders.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Plans, manages and investigates business situation analysis where there is significant ambiguity and complexity. Advises on the approach and techniques to be used. Ensures holistic view adopted to identify and analyze wide-ranging problems and opportunities. Engages and collaborates with a wide range of stakeholders, including those at the management level.

**Level 6 (Initiate, influence):**
Initiates and leads business situation analysis where there is extensive ambiguity, complexity and potentially significant organizational impacts. Establishes and promotes the need for holistic business situation analysis prior to change program initiation. Engages with stakeholders at executive level and advises on recommended change initiatives.

### Feasibility assessment (FEAS)

Defining, evaluating and describing business change options for financial, technical and business feasibility.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Supports option identification and feasibility assessment. Selects and employs standard techniques to get the information required. Supports identification of tangible costs and benefits, and development of business cases.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Selects relevant feasibility assessment approaches and techniques. Identifies the range of possible options. Undertakes short-listing of options and feasibility assessment. Engages with internal and external stakeholders. Supports preparation of business cases including cost/benefit, impact and risk analysis for each option.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Manages investigative work to enable feasibility assessments. Collaborates with stakeholders and specialists to get the information required. Advises on the selection of feasibility assessment approaches and techniques relevant to the business situation. Prepares business cases, including cost/benefit, impact and risk analysis for each option.

**Level 6 (Initiate, influence):**
Establishes an organizational framework and standards for feasibility assessment and business case development. Directs and leads feasibility assessments for initiatives that will have a significant impact on the organization. Presents feasibility assessments and business cases to senior stakeholders.

### Requirements definition and management (REQM)

Managing requirements through the entire delivery and operational lifecycle.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Defines and manages scoping, requirements definition and prioritization activities for small-scale changes. Follows agreed standards and applies appropriate techniques to elicit and document detailed requirements. Provides constructive challenge to stakeholders. Reviews requirements for errors and omissions. Prioritizes requirements and documents traceability to source.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Defines and manages scoping, requirements definition and prioritization activities for initiatives of medium size and complexity. Contributes to selecting the requirements approach. Facilitates input from stakeholders, provides constructive challenge and enables effective prioritization. Establishes requirements baselines or backlogs, obtains appropriate agreement.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Plans and drives scoping, requirements definition and prioritization activities for large, complex initiatives. Selects, adopts and adapts appropriate requirements definition and management methods, tools and techniques. Obtains input and agreement from a diverse range of stakeholders. Negotiates with stakeholders to manage competing priorities and conflicts.

**Level 6 (Initiate, influence):**
Champions the importance and value of requirements management principles. Develops organizational policies, standards and guidelines. Plans and leads scoping, requirements definition and priority setting for complex, strategic programs. Drives adoption of, and adherence to, policies and standards.

### Methods and tools (METL)

Ensuring that appropriate methods and tools for planning, development, testing and operation are adopted and used effectively.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Provides support on the use of existing methods and tools. Configures methods and tools within a known context. Creates and updates the documentation of methods and tools.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Provides advice and guidance to support adoption of methods and tools and adherence to policies and standards. Tailors methods and tools for use in projects and teams. Assesses the impact of change to methods and tools. Implements improvements to methods and tools.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Provides advice and guidance on the use of methods, techniques and tools for planning, development, testing and operation. Advises on available standards, methods, tools, applications and processes and can make appropriate choices from alternatives. Contributes to organizational policies, standards and guidelines for methods and tools.

### Business process improvement (BPRE)

Creating new and potentially disruptive approaches to performing business activities.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Applies standard techniques to analyze existing business processes and identifies opportunities for improvement. Collaborates with stakeholders to ensure process changes align with business objectives. Proposes and implements process improvements. Develops and maintains process documentation.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Analyzes and designs business processes to identify alternative solutions to improve efficiency, effectiveness and exploit new technologies and automation. Develops graphical models of business processes to facilitate understanding and decision-making. Recommends implementation approaches for process improvement initiatives.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Manages the execution of business process improvements. Assesses the feasibility of business process changes and recommends alternative approaches. Selects, tailors and implements methods and tools for improving business processes. Contributes to the definition of organizational policies, standards and guidelines.

### Data modelling and design (DTAN)

Developing models and diagrams to represent and communicate data requirements and data assets.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Applies standard modelling techniques based on pre-determined scope and objectives to develop data models. Interprets data models and feeds back results to senior analysts.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Investigates corporate data requirements to establish data needs. Plans data modelling activities. Creates detailed data modelling deliverables. Applies data modelling techniques to provide detailed data requirements. Assists with defining data standards and developing data governance processes.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Plans and manages data modelling and design activities for complex initiatives. Sets standards for data modelling and design. Reviews data models to ensure adherence to standards. Advises on the selection and use of modelling techniques. Contributes to organizational data governance frameworks.

### Acceptance testing (BPTS)

Validating systems, products, business processes or services to determine whether the acceptance criteria have been satisfied.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Designs test cases and test scripts under own direction, mapping back to pre-determined criteria, recording and reporting outcomes. Participates in requirements, design and specification reviews. Applies agreed standards and tools.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Designs test cases and creates test scripts and supporting data, working to the overall acceptance testing strategy. Defines, analyzes and refines acceptance criteria, working with stakeholders. Interprets, executes and records test cases. Analyzes and reports test activities and results. Identifies and reports issues and risks.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Takes responsibility for the management, design and execution of acceptance testing, including test process improvements. Manages the acceptance testing process. Develops acceptance testing strategies. Obtains sign-off for testing activities. Selects methods, tools and techniques. Contributes to organizational testing standards.

### User experience analysis (UNAN)

Understanding the context of use and defining measurable requirements for user experience and accessibility.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Applies standard techniques to identify and document the user experience requirements for systems, products and services. Assists in developing measurable criteria for user experience.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Identifies and specifies user experience requirements and related organizational objectives. Selects appropriate analysis methods and tools. Specifies measurable criteria for user experience. Develops recommendations for improving user satisfaction.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Plans and manages user experience analysis activities. Advises on the selection and implementation of methods and tools. Defines organizational standards for user experience analysis. Ensures user experience requirements are integrated into overall solution design.

### Business modelling (BSMO)

Producing abstract representations of real-world business situations to aid communication and understanding.

**Level 3 (Apply):**
Creates business models using standard notations and tools under direction. Validates models with stakeholders.

**Level 4 (Enable):**
Creates and maintains business models using recognized notations and approaches. Selects appropriate modelling techniques. Interprets models for stakeholders and facilitates understanding. Ensures alignment between business models and requirements.

**Level 5 (Ensure, advise):**
Leads business modelling activities for complex initiatives. Advises on the selection and adaptation of modelling approaches. Ensures consistency and traceability between models. Contributes to organizational standards for business modelling.

## Secondary Skills

### Enterprise and business architecture (STPL)

Creating, iterating and maintaining strategies and architectural roadmaps.

**Level 4:** Contributes to the development of architectures in specific business or functional areas. Identifies and evaluates alternative architectures and the trade-offs. Documents architecturally significant decisions.

**Level 5:** Leads the development of architectures in specific areas. Provides guidance and governance on solution development. Ensures relevant strategies, policies, standards and practices are applied correctly.

### Consultancy (CNSL)

Providing advice and recommendations based on expertise and experience.

**Level 4:** Takes responsibility for specific engagement elements. Collaborates with clients and client representatives. Facilitates resolution of issues.

**Level 5:** Takes full responsibility for understanding client requirements. Manages scope and delivery of engagements. Ensures the client can fully exploit the proposed solution.

### Stakeholder relationship management (RLMT)

Systematically analyzing, managing and influencing stakeholder relationships.

**Level 4:** Implements stakeholder engagement/communications plans. Collects and uses feedback. Helps develop and enhance customer and stakeholder relationships.

**Level 5:** Identifies communication needs of stakeholder groups. Facilitates open communication. Acts as a single point of contact. Facilitates business decision-making processes.

### Organisational change management (CIPM)

Planning, designing, and implementing activities to transition the organization and people to the required future state.

**Level 4:** Assesses and monitors the impact of organizational change. Supports the development of change management plans. Coordinates change implementation activities.

**Level 5:** Develops and implements change management strategies. Manages the transition process. Assesses organizational readiness. Leads stakeholder engagement during transitions.

### Benefits management (BENM)

Forecasting, planning and monitoring the emergence and effective realisation of anticipated benefits.

**Level 4:** Monitors the emergence and realization of benefits. Supports the development of benefits realization plans. Reports on progress against planned benefits.

**Level 5:** Develops and manages benefits realization plans. Establishes metrics and tracking mechanisms. Advises stakeholders on benefits management approaches.

### Innovation management (INOV)

Identifying, prioritizing, incubating and exploiting opportunities.

**Level 5:** Manages the innovation pipeline and executes innovation processes. Develops and adapts innovation tools. Encourages and motivates innovation communities to share creative ideas and learn from failures.

### Strategic planning (ITSP)

Creating and maintaining a strategy to align organizational actions with business objectives.

**Level 4:** Contributes to the development of strategies within specific areas. Gathers and analyzes information to inform strategic decisions.

**Level 5:** Leads strategy development in specific areas. Aligns strategic plans with business objectives. Advises on strategic options and their implications.

### Demand management (DEMM)

Analyzing and proactively managing business demand for new services or modifications.

**Level 4:** Performs demand management analysis. Monitors patterns of demand. Identifies and assesses opportunities to prioritize alignment between business demand and capacity.

**Level 5:** Implements demand management analysis and planning. Manages the process of integrating demand management with strategic and operational processes.

## Level Transition Summary

| Transition | Key Shift |
|-----------|-----------|
| L3 → L4 | From following techniques to selecting and applying them. From straightforward to complex/ambiguous situations. From documenting to facilitating and challenging. |
| L4 → L5 | From managing parts to leading complex initiatives. From applying methods to selecting, adapting, and advising on them. From operational to strategic stakeholder engagement. From doing to establishing standards. |
| L5 → L6 | From leading initiatives to establishing organizational frameworks. From advising to championing. From strategic engagement to executive-level influence. |
