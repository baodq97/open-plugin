# PM Phases -- Detailed Guide

## Phase 1: Discover

### Purpose
Understand the project context, identify stakeholders, assess the business case, and clarify what the project needs to deliver.

### Conversation Guide

Start with these questions (adapt to context):

1. **Problem space**: "What problem or opportunity is this project addressing? What triggered this initiative?"
2. **Stakeholders**: "Who are the key stakeholders? Who sponsors this project? Who will be impacted?"
3. **Business case**: "What is the expected business value? Has an investment appraisal been done?"
4. **Existing landscape**: "What is already in place? Are there related projects or programmes?"
5. **Constraints**: "What are the hard constraints? (budget, timeline, regulatory, resource availability)"
6. **Success criteria**: "How will we know the project has been successful? What are the key outcomes?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Identify all stakeholders and their concerns
- [ ] Document project context and business drivers
- [ ] Summarize the business case or investment rationale
- [ ] Map related projects, programmes, or portfolio context
- [ ] Identify quality and success criteria
- [ ] Document known constraints and assumptions

### Artifacts

**Project Brief** (`discover/project-brief.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Capture: project purpose, business drivers, high-level scope, expected outcomes, constraints

**Stakeholder Register** (`discover/stakeholder-register.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Capture: name/role, interest, influence level, engagement strategy

**Business Case Summary** (`discover/business-case-summary.md`)
- Investment rationale
- Expected benefits (quantified where possible)
- High-level cost estimate
- Key assumptions and risks

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 4 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Project management | PRMG | Identify, assess and manage risks effectively |
| Stakeholder relationship management | RLMT | Deal with problems and issues, manage resolutions |
| Investment appraisal | INVA | Develop and document investment appraisals, identify suitable appraisal techniques |

### Coaching Moments

- After stakeholder identification: coach on completeness (did they miss any stakeholder groups?)
- After business case: coach on quantifying benefits, not just listing them
- Before transitioning: coach on what "good enough" discovery looks like for the project scale

### When to Skip

Skip Discover if:
- Project brief and business case are already well-documented
- This is a well-understood domain with clear stakeholders and mandate
- The task is a sub-project within an established programme

## Phase 2: Define

### Purpose
Formalize project scope, establish governance structure, define success criteria, and create the project charter.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Scope**: "What is in scope vs. out of scope? What are the key deliverables?"
2. **Governance**: "What governance framework will we use? Who approves what?"
3. **Success criteria**: "What are the measurable success criteria? How will we track benefits?"
4. **Roles**: "Who is responsible for what? Let's build a RACI matrix."
5. **Dependencies**: "What does this project depend on? What depends on this project?"
6. **Approach**: "What methodology is most appropriate? (Waterfall, Agile, Hybrid)"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Create project charter with clear scope, objectives, and authority
- [ ] Define governance framework and decision-making process
- [ ] Create benefits plan with measurable criteria
- [ ] Build RACI matrix for key activities
- [ ] Identify dependencies on other projects or programmes
- [ ] Select project methodology and approach

### Artifacts

**Project Charter** (`define/project-charter.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Formal authorization document: objectives, scope, governance, authority, constraints

**Governance Framework** (`define/governance-framework.md`)
- Steering committee structure
- Decision-making authority levels
- Escalation paths
- Reporting cadence

**Benefits Plan** (`define/benefits-plan.md`)
- Expected benefits mapped to business objectives
- Measurable KPIs for each benefit
- Benefits realization timeline
- Benefits owner per benefit

**RACI Matrix** (`define/raci-matrix.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Key activities mapped to Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 4 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Project management | PRMG | Define, document and execute projects, prepare realistic project plans |
| Portfolio/programme/project support | PROF | Support project control boards, provide guidance on PM processes |
| Benefits management | BENM | Contribute to benefits management plans, engage stakeholders to identify and quantify benefits |

### Coaching Moments

- After charter: coach on ensuring the charter has clear authority and escalation paths
- After RACI: coach on avoiding ambiguity -- every key activity needs exactly one Accountable
- After governance: coach on proportionality -- governance should match project complexity

### When to Skip

Skip Define if:
- Project charter and governance are already established
- Working within a programme that provides governance
- Iterating on an existing project with known structure

## Phase 3: Plan

### Purpose
Create detailed project plans, allocate resources, establish budgets, identify risks, and set up communication channels.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Work breakdown**: "What are the major work packages? Can we break them into manageable tasks?"
2. **Dependencies**: "What are the task dependencies? What is the critical path?"
3. **Resources**: "What resources do we need? (people, skills, tools, infrastructure)"
4. **Budget**: "What is the budget? How do we track costs against plan?"
5. **Risks**: "What are the top risks? What is the RAID log status?"
6. **Communication**: "Who needs to know what, when, and how?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Create work breakdown structure (WBS)
- [ ] Identify task dependencies and critical path
- [ ] Create resource plan with skill requirements
- [ ] Establish budget and cost tracking mechanisms
- [ ] Build risk register (RAID log)
- [ ] Create communication plan
- [ ] Define measurement approach and KPIs
- [ ] Establish milestone schedule

### Artifacts

**Project Plan (WBS)** (`plan/project-plan.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Work breakdown structure with dependencies, estimates, and milestones

**Resource Plan** (`plan/resource-plan.md`)
- Resource requirements by phase/work package
- Skill matrix and availability
- Resource acquisition approach

**Budget/Cost Plan** (`plan/budget.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Cost breakdown by work package
- Contingency allocation
- Cost tracking approach

**Risk Register (RAID Log)** (`plan/raid-log.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies
- Likelihood, impact, mitigation strategies

**Communication Plan** (`plan/communication-plan.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Stakeholder communication needs
- Reporting cadence and format
- Escalation procedures

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 4 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Project management | PRMG | Prepare realistic project plans including scope, schedule, quality, risk and communication plans |
| Demand management | DEMM | Perform demand management analysis and planning within a specific area |
| Financial management | FMIT | Monitor and maintain financial records, support decision-making by collating financial information |
| Measurement | MEAS | Identify and prioritize appropriate measures, scales and targets |

### Coaching Moments

- After WBS: coach on appropriate level of detail (not too granular, not too vague)
- After risk register: coach on being honest about risks, not hiding them to look good
- After budget: coach on contingency planning -- every budget needs a contingency reserve
- Before transitioning: coach on ensuring the plan is a living document, not a one-time artifact

## Phase 4: Execute

### Purpose
Monitor and control project delivery, manage changes, resolve issues, report progress, and maintain stakeholder engagement.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Progress**: "What has been completed? What is behind schedule?"
2. **Issues**: "What issues have arisen? How are they being managed?"
3. **Changes**: "Are there any change requests? What is the impact on scope/time/cost?"
4. **Stakeholders**: "How are stakeholders engaged? Any concerns or escalations?"
5. **Risks**: "Have any risks materialized? Are there new risks?"
6. **Quality**: "Are deliverables meeting quality standards?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Track progress against milestones and plan
- [ ] Produce regular status reports
- [ ] Manage change requests through change control process
- [ ] Maintain issue log and drive resolution
- [ ] Update risk register with new and changed risks
- [ ] Conduct stakeholder engagement activities
- [ ] Monitor costs against budget
- [ ] Conduct quality reviews

### Artifacts

**Status Report** (`execute/status-report.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- RAG status, progress summary, risks and issues, upcoming milestones

**Change Request** (`execute/change-requests/CR-NNN-*.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Change description, impact analysis, approval status

**Issue Log** (`execute/issue-log.md`)
- Active issues with owner, priority, resolution plan
- Escalation status

**Milestone Tracker** (`execute/milestone-tracker.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Planned vs actual dates, variance analysis

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 4 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Project management | PRMG | Track activities against schedule, monitor costs, times, quality and resources |
| Information systems coordination | ISCO | Coordinate and collaborate on implementation of information systems and services |
| Organisational change management | CIPM | Conduct readiness assessments, develop and communicate tailored change management plans |
| Stakeholder relationship management | RLMT | Implement stakeholder engagement/communications plans, collect and use feedback |

### Coaching Moments

- After status report: coach on honest reporting -- escalate early, not late
- After change request: coach on impact analysis -- every change affects scope, time, or cost
- After issue resolution: coach on capturing lessons -- every issue is a learning opportunity
- During execution: coach on maintaining focus on outcomes, not just activities

## Phase 5: Close & Learn

### Purpose
Formally close the project, hand over deliverables, review benefits realization, and capture lessons learned.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Completion**: "Have all deliverables been accepted? Are there outstanding items?"
2. **Handover**: "Who is receiving the deliverables? Is the operational team ready?"
3. **Benefits**: "Have the expected benefits been realized? How do we measure ongoing benefits?"
4. **Lessons**: "What went well? What would we do differently? What surprised us?"
5. **Governance**: "Has the project been formally closed? Have resources been released?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Verify all deliverables are complete and accepted
- [ ] Create handover document for operational team
- [ ] Conduct benefits review against the benefits plan
- [ ] Facilitate lessons learned session
- [ ] Create project closure report
- [ ] Release resources and close financial accounts
- [ ] Archive project documentation
- [ ] Obtain formal project closure sign-off

### Artifacts

**Project Closure Report** (`close-learn/project-closure-report.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Final status, deliverables summary, budget variance, key decisions, outstanding items

**Benefits Realization Report** (`close-learn/benefits-realization-report.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- Expected vs actual benefits
- Ongoing measurement plan for deferred benefits

**Lessons Learned** (`close-learn/lessons-learned.md`)
- Use template from `artifact-templates.md`
- What went well, what to improve, recommendations for future projects

**Handover Document** (`close-learn/handover-document.md`)
- Deliverables inventory
- Operational procedures
- Support arrangements
- Known issues and workarounds

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 4 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Project management | PRMG | Take action where costs, times, quality and resources exceed agreed tolerances |
| Benefits management | BENM | Monitor and report on progress towards benefits realization |
| Measurement | MEAS | Specify how to collect and store data for each required measure |
| Methods and tools | METL | Recommend appropriate solutions, provide advice on adoption |

### Coaching Moments

- After closure report: coach on completeness -- ensure nothing is left in limbo
- After benefits review: coach on separating delivered benefits from deferred benefits
- After lessons learned: coach on being specific -- "improve communication" is not actionable
- At completion: summarize SFIA skills practiced and level-up opportunities

### When to Skip

Close & Learn should never be fully skipped, but can be streamlined for small projects:
- Small projects: combine closure report and lessons learned into a single document
- Sub-projects: feed lessons into the parent programme's lessons log
