# Product Leadership Competency Assessment Criteria

## Assessment Dimensions

### 1. Completeness
Does the work cover all necessary aspects?

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **L4** | Covers basic elements with some gaps. Follows templates without adaptation. Focuses on individual product scope. |
| **L5** | Covers all required elements for the context. Adapts templates to fit the situation. Includes stakeholder input. Considers cross-product implications. |
| **L6** | Comprehensive coverage with proactive identification of gaps. Anticipates future needs. Integrates portfolio-wide perspective. Addresses organizational-level concerns. |
| **L7** | Sets the standard for completeness across the organization. Creates frameworks others can follow. Addresses enterprise-wide strategic concerns and industry implications. |

### 2. Depth
How deep is the analysis and thinking?

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **L4** | Surface-level analysis. Identifies obvious issues. Documents what is provided. |
| **L5** | Meaningful analysis with root cause thinking. Identifies non-obvious trade-offs. Considers multiple options. Synthesizes data from multiple sources. |
| **L6** | Strategic depth across multiple domains. Identifies emerging trends and second-order effects. Shapes organizational direction. Establishes policies and frameworks. |
| **L7** | Enterprise-level strategic depth. Anticipates industry-level shifts. Shapes organizational vision and culture. Directs strategic frameworks. |

### 3. Communication
How effectively is the work communicated?

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **L4** | Clear documentation for peers. Uses standard formats. Presents information logically. |
| **L5** | Communicates with impact across stakeholder groups. Facilitates decision-making. Builds consensus. Adapts message for different audiences. |
| **L6** | Communicates with authority at all organizational levels including board. Influences strategy. Promotes knowledge sharing. Shapes organizational narrative. |
| **L7** | Communicates to industry-level audiences. Presents compelling strategic vision authoritatively. Inspires organizational alignment. |

### 4. Decision Quality
How well are decisions made and justified?

| Level | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| **L4** | Makes data-informed decisions with clear trade-offs. Considers alternatives. Justifies choices with rationale. |
| **L5** | Makes strategic decisions balancing competing stakeholder priorities. Manages uncertainty. Drives prioritization actively with market evidence. |
| **L6** | Makes decisions that shape organizational strategy. Balances portfolio-level trade-offs. Establishes decision-making frameworks. Initiates strategic change. |
| **L7** | Makes decisions critical to organizational strategic direction. Defines the decision-making framework itself. Balances enterprise-wide considerations. |

## Skill-Specific Assessment Indicators

### Product Management (PROD)

| Indicator | L5 | L6 | L7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Portfolio management | Manages full product lifecycle | Oversees portfolio, creates frameworks | Defines strategic management framework |
| Strategy | Develops product propositions and positioning | Aligns product objectives with business objectives | Directs strategy creation and review |
| Lifecycle management | Manages full lifecycle including retirement | Creates lifecycle management framework | Sets organizational approach to lifecycle |
| Innovation | Adapts products based on changing needs | Initiates creation of new products and services | Identifies new opportunities at enterprise level |

### Strategic Planning (ITSP)

| Indicator | L5 | L6 | L7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Strategy development | Contributes to strategy, develops plans | Sets policies, leads strategy creation | Leads strategic management framework |
| Communication | Communicates plans, ensures stakeholder awareness | Implements strategic management processes | Directs creation and review of strategy |
| Policy | Contributes to policies and guidelines | Sets policies and standards | Leads definition and implementation |

### Stakeholder Relationship Management (RLMT)

| Indicator | L5 | L6 | L7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Engagement | Develops strategies, acts as single POC | Leads comprehensive strategies, builds long-term relationships | Determines strategic approach, ultimate escalation point |
| Communication | Facilitates discussion and decision-making | Ensures effective communication and alignment | Establishes vision for stakeholder management |
| Negotiation | Facilitates business decisions | Negotiates agreements at executive level | Manages most senior stakeholder relationships |

### Market Research (MRCH)

| Indicator | L5 | L6 | L7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Research leadership | Leads complex studies, synthesizes sources | Sets research direction and strategy | — |
| Strategic impact | Presents findings and recommendations | Communicates insights to senior leadership | — |
| Organizational capability | Contributes to research standards | Develops policies, aligns capabilities with needs | — |

### Innovation Management (INOV)

| Indicator | L5 | L6 | L7 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Pipeline | Manages innovation pipeline | Develops organizational innovation capabilities | Embeds innovation across business units |
| Culture | Encourages creative ideas, learns from failures | Obtains organizational commitment to innovation | Leads development of innovation culture |
| Process | Develops and adapts innovation tools | Leads implementation of processes and frameworks | Aligns objectives and rewards with innovation |

## Quick Assessment Checklist

For rapid assessment during a CPO session:

### Strategic Thinking
- [ ] Articulates clear portfolio vision grounded in market evidence
- [ ] Connects portfolio decisions to organizational strategy
- [ ] Identifies second-order effects of portfolio choices
- [ ] Balances short-term performance with long-term positioning

### Portfolio Management
- [ ] Makes explicit investment trade-offs with rationale
- [ ] Uses a consistent prioritization framework at portfolio level
- [ ] Has courage to sunset underperforming products
- [ ] Manages innovation pipeline alongside core portfolio

### Market Orientation
- [ ] Grounds decisions in market research and customer evidence
- [ ] Monitors competitive landscape and industry trends
- [ ] Validates strategic assumptions with data
- [ ] Adapts strategy based on market feedback

### Stakeholder Leadership
- [ ] Communicates effectively at board and C-suite level
- [ ] Builds strategic relationships that transcend individual products
- [ ] Negotiates competing priorities across the organization
- [ ] Partners effectively with CTO/engineering leadership

### Organizational Capability
- [ ] Builds product management capability across the organization
- [ ] Establishes governance frameworks that enable, not constrain
- [ ] Champions product culture and talent development
- [ ] Creates systems and frameworks for scalable product management
