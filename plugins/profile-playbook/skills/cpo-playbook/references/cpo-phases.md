# CPO Phases — Detailed Guide

## Phase 1: Discover

### Purpose
Understand the market landscape, competitive position, customer segments, and industry trends to ground product strategy in evidence.

### Conversation Guide

Start with these questions (adapt to context):

1. **Market landscape**: "What is the current market landscape? What macro trends are shaping your industry? What disruptions are emerging?"
2. **Competitive position**: "Who are the key competitors? What is your competitive moat? Where are you winning and losing?"
3. **Customer segments**: "Who are your most valuable customer segments? How are their needs evolving? What segments are underserved?"
4. **Industry trends**: "What technology, regulatory, or behavioral shifts will impact your product portfolio in the next 2-5 years?"
5. **Existing portfolio**: "What products/services do you currently offer? What is the revenue and growth profile of each?"
6. **Strategic vision**: "What does success look like for the product organization in 12-24 months?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Conduct market landscape analysis (size, growth, trends, disruptions)
- [ ] Build competitive positioning matrix (competitors, strengths, weaknesses, differentiation)
- [ ] Map customer segments with needs, value, and growth potential
- [ ] Identify industry trends and their portfolio implications
- [ ] Assess current portfolio health (revenue, growth, market share per product)
- [ ] Document strategic assumptions and unknowns

### Artifacts

**Market Landscape Analysis** (`discover/market-landscape.md`)
- Market size and growth trajectory
- Key trends and disruptions
- Regulatory and technology shifts
- Opportunity areas

**Competitive Positioning Matrix** (`discover/competitive-positioning.md`)
- Competitor profiles with strengths and weaknesses
- Differentiation factors per product line
- Competitive gaps and opportunities

**Customer Segment Map** (`discover/customer-segments.md`)
- Segment profiles with demographics, needs, value
- Segment prioritization (size, growth, strategic fit)
- Underserved segments and white space

**Industry Trend Report** (`discover/industry-trends.md`)
- Technology trends impacting the portfolio
- Regulatory changes on the horizon
- Behavioral shifts in target markets
- Implications for product strategy

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 5 Activity | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|
| Market research | MRCH | Lead complex market research, synthesize multiple sources, present strategic recommendations | Set direction for market research, develop organizational policies, communicate insights to senior leadership |
| Business situation analysis | BUSA | Plan and manage analysis with significant ambiguity, advise on approach, engage management-level stakeholders | Initiate and lead analysis with extensive complexity, engage executive-level stakeholders, define organizational policies |
| Stakeholder relationship management | RLMT | Identify communication needs, facilitate discussion, act as single point of contact | Lead comprehensive stakeholder strategies, build long-term strategic relationships |

### Coaching Moments

- After market landscape: coach on distinguishing signal from noise — which trends will actually impact the portfolio?
- After competitive positioning: coach on identifying true differentiation vs. feature parity
- After customer segments: coach on making hard choices — not every segment is strategic
- Before transitioning: coach on what "good enough" discovery looks like at the strategic level

### When to Skip

Skip Discover if:
- Recent market analysis exists (e.g., from strategy consulting or board materials)
- This is a portfolio rebalancing exercise with known market context
- The task is focused on internal portfolio optimization, not market positioning

## Phase 2: Define

### Purpose
Define the product portfolio vision, articulate the strategy, establish success metrics, and create the governance framework for product decisions.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Portfolio vision**: "What is the overarching vision for the product portfolio? What future state are you creating for customers and the market?"
2. **Strategy**: "What is the product strategy? Platform-led, product-led, or customer-led? What is the investment thesis?"
3. **Success metrics**: "How will you measure portfolio success? What are the portfolio-level OKRs?"
4. **Governance**: "How are product decisions made? Who has authority for what? What is the product investment review cadence?"
5. **Boundaries**: "What is the product organization responsible for? What explicitly belongs to engineering, sales, or other functions?"
6. **Culture**: "What product culture do you want to build? How do you develop product talent?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Write product portfolio vision statement
- [ ] Create product strategy document with investment thesis
- [ ] Define portfolio-level OKRs and success metrics
- [ ] Establish product governance framework (decision rights, review cadence, escalation)
- [ ] Define product organization principles and boundaries
- [ ] Articulate the product management operating model

### Artifacts

**Product Portfolio Vision** (`define/portfolio-vision.md`)
- Vision statement for the entire product portfolio
- Strategic pillars and themes
- Target market position (3-5 year horizon)
- Portfolio composition goals

**Product Strategy Document** (`define/product-strategy.md`)
- Strategy narrative and investment thesis
- Portfolio strategy approach (platform-led, product-led, customer-led)
- Growth levers and strategic bets
- Risk appetite and constraints

**Portfolio OKRs** (`define/portfolio-okrs.md`)
- Portfolio-level objectives and key results
- Per-product contribution to portfolio OKRs
- Leading and lagging indicators
- Measurement and review cadence

**Product Governance Framework** (`define/governance-framework.md`)
- Decision rights matrix (who decides what)
- Product investment review process
- Stage-gate or continuous funding model
- Escalation paths and conflict resolution

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 5 Activity | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|
| Product management | PROD | Manage full product lifecycle, select and adapt methods, develop propositions | Oversee portfolio, create lifecycle management framework, champion product management principles |
| Strategic planning | ITSP | Collate information, develop and communicate plans, contribute to policy development | Set policies and guidelines, lead strategy creation, implement strategic management processes |
| Governance | GOVN | — | Implement governance framework, determine requirements, communicate delegated authority |

### Coaching Moments

- After portfolio vision: coach on making the vision inspiring yet grounded in market evidence
- After strategy document: coach on distinguishing strategy (choices) from goals (aspirations)
- After OKRs: coach on portfolio-level measurement — not just rolling up product metrics
- Before transitioning: coach on governance as enablement, not bureaucracy

### When to Skip

Skip Define if:
- Portfolio vision and strategy already exist and are current
- Board-approved strategy is in place, focus is on execution
- The task is a tactical portfolio rebalancing within existing strategy

## Phase 3: Prioritize

### Purpose
Manage the product portfolio, allocate investment across products and initiatives, make explicit trade-offs, and manage the innovation pipeline.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Portfolio composition**: "What products are in the portfolio? How do they map to strategic pillars?"
2. **Investment allocation**: "How is investment currently distributed? What is the right balance between core, adjacent, and transformational?"
3. **Trade-offs**: "What products or initiatives are you willing to sunset, reduce, or defer? What gets more investment?"
4. **Innovation pipeline**: "What new opportunities are in the pipeline? How do you evaluate and incubate them?"
5. **Dependencies**: "What cross-product dependencies exist? Where do portfolio-level trade-offs need to be made?"
6. **Stakeholder alignment**: "Do the leadership team and board agree on portfolio priorities? Where are the tensions?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Map current portfolio to strategic pillars
- [ ] Apply portfolio prioritization framework
- [ ] Create investment allocation plan (core/adjacent/transformational or similar)
- [ ] Build innovation pipeline with evaluation criteria
- [ ] Conduct opportunity assessment for new initiatives
- [ ] Document trade-off decisions with rationale
- [ ] Align leadership team on portfolio priorities

### Artifacts

**Portfolio Prioritization Matrix** (`prioritize/prioritization-matrix.md`)
- Products/initiatives mapped against strategic value and market attractiveness
- Prioritization framework and scoring rationale
- Invest/maintain/harvest/sunset recommendations

**Investment Allocation Plan** (`prioritize/investment-allocation.md`)
- Budget allocation across products and strategic pillars
- Core/adjacent/transformational split
- Investment criteria and thresholds
- Rebalancing triggers

**Innovation Pipeline** (`prioritize/innovation-pipeline.md`)
- Pipeline of new opportunities and bets
- Evaluation criteria and stage-gate process
- Resource allocation for exploration
- Success criteria for graduation

**Opportunity Assessment** (`prioritize/opportunity-assessment.md`)
- New opportunity evaluation template
- Market size, competitive landscape, strategic fit
- Required investment and expected return
- Risk assessment and go/no-go recommendation

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 5 Activity | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|
| Product management | PROD | Manage full lifecycle, prioritize requirements, develop roadmaps | Oversee portfolio, align product objectives with business objectives, initiate new products |
| Business situation analysis | BUSA | Plan and manage complex analysis, advise on approach, engage management stakeholders | Initiate and lead analysis with significant organizational impact, engage executive stakeholders |
| Innovation management | INOV | Manage innovation pipeline, develop tools and processes, identify resources needed | Obtain organizational commitment, develop capabilities, lead innovation process implementation |

### Coaching Moments

- After prioritization matrix: coach on the courage to sunset — not every product deserves continued investment
- After investment allocation: coach on balancing exploitation (core) with exploration (transformational)
- After innovation pipeline: coach on creating safe-to-fail experiments, not just safe bets
- Before transitioning: coach on communicating tough portfolio decisions to stakeholders

### When to Skip

Skip Prioritize if:
- Portfolio is small (1-2 products) with clear priorities
- Investment allocation is fixed and non-negotiable
- This is a single-product strategy exercise, not portfolio management

## Phase 4: Plan

### Purpose
Coordinate cross-product roadmaps, align stakeholders on execution, manage dependencies between products, and develop go-to-market strategies.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Portfolio roadmap**: "What is the sequencing of major initiatives across the portfolio? What are the key milestones?"
2. **Dependencies**: "What cross-product dependencies exist? What shared platforms or capabilities are needed?"
3. **Stakeholder alignment**: "Who are the key stakeholders (board, C-suite, engineering, sales)? What does each need from the product organization?"
4. **Go-to-market**: "What is the go-to-market approach? Product-led growth, sales-led, partner-led, or hybrid?"
5. **Resource planning**: "Do you have the right product talent and organizational structure to execute?"
6. **Risk management**: "What are the strategic risks? What happens if a key bet fails?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Create portfolio roadmap with cross-product milestones
- [ ] Map cross-product dependencies and shared capabilities
- [ ] Develop stakeholder alignment plan (board, C-suite, engineering, sales)
- [ ] Define go-to-market strategy for key initiatives
- [ ] Assess organizational readiness and talent gaps
- [ ] Identify strategic risks and mitigation strategies

### Artifacts

**Portfolio Roadmap** (`plan/portfolio-roadmap.md`)
- Cross-product timeline with major milestones
- Strategic themes per quarter/half
- Shared capability delivery schedule
- Decision points and review gates

**Cross-Product Dependency Map** (`plan/dependency-map.md`)
- Product-to-product dependencies
- Shared platform and capability requirements
- Sequencing constraints
- Critical path analysis

**Stakeholder Alignment Plan** (`plan/stakeholder-alignment.md`)
- Stakeholder influence/interest matrix at executive level
- Communication cadence per stakeholder group
- Board reporting structure and content
- Escalation and decision-making paths

**Go-to-Market Strategy** (`plan/go-to-market.md`)
- GTM approach per product/initiative
- Channel strategy (direct, partner, community)
- Launch sequencing and market entry timing
- Success metrics and market feedback loops

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 5 Activity | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|
| Stakeholder relationship management | RLMT | Identify communication needs, facilitate decision-making, develop engagement strategies | Lead comprehensive strategies, build long-term strategic relationships, negotiate agreements |
| Strategic planning | ITSP | Develop and communicate plans, contribute to policy, support stakeholder adherence | Set policies, lead strategy creation, implement strategic management in operational management |
| Solution architecture | ARCH | Lead solution architecture development, provide technical guidance and governance | Lead architecture for complex solutions, establish policies and principles, manage trade-offs |

### Coaching Moments

- After portfolio roadmap: coach on communicating uncertainty at the strategic level — roadmaps are hypotheses
- After dependency map: coach on reducing coupling between products for organizational agility
- After stakeholder alignment: coach on managing the board — what they need vs. what they ask for
- Before transitioning: coach on the difference between a plan (adaptable) and a commitment (fixed)

### When to Skip

Skip Plan if:
- Execution planning is delegated to individual product teams
- Focus is on strategy definition, not execution planning
- Organizational structure handles cross-product coordination autonomously

## Phase 5: Deliver & Learn

### Purpose
Monitor portfolio performance, synthesize market feedback, conduct strategic retrospectives, and iterate the product strategy.

### Conversation Guide

1. **Portfolio performance**: "How is the portfolio performing against OKRs? Which products are on track, which are underperforming?"
2. **Market feedback**: "What is the market telling you? What has changed since you defined the strategy?"
3. **Strategic learning**: "What strategic bets are paying off? Which ones need to pivot or be abandoned?"
4. **Organization health**: "How is the product organization performing? Where are the talent gaps or cultural issues?"
5. **Strategy refresh**: "Based on learnings, what adjustments are needed to the portfolio strategy?"
6. **Next cycle**: "What changes to investment allocation, priorities, or organizational structure for the next cycle?"

### Activities Checklist

- [ ] Review portfolio performance against OKRs and success metrics
- [ ] Synthesize market feedback and competitive intelligence updates
- [ ] Conduct strategic retrospective on portfolio decisions
- [ ] Assess product organization health and talent development
- [ ] Update portfolio strategy based on learnings
- [ ] Prepare strategy refresh for leadership and board
- [ ] Plan next strategy cycle

### Artifacts

**Portfolio Performance Dashboard** (`deliver-learn/performance-dashboard.md`)
- Portfolio-level metrics vs. targets
- Per-product performance summary
- Investment ROI analysis
- Leading indicators and trend analysis

**Market Feedback Synthesis** (`deliver-learn/market-feedback.md`)
- Customer feedback themes across products
- Competitive landscape changes
- Market shifts and new opportunities
- Implications for portfolio strategy

**Strategic Retrospective** (`deliver-learn/strategic-retrospective.md`)
- Strategic decisions reviewed with outcomes
- What worked and what did not
- Root cause analysis for underperformance
- Strategic lessons learned

**Strategy Refresh** (`deliver-learn/strategy-refresh.md`)
- Updated portfolio vision and strategy (if changed)
- Revised investment allocation
- New priorities and sunset decisions
- Updated OKRs for next cycle

### SFIA Skills Practiced

| Skill | Code | Level 5 Activity | Level 6 Activity |
|-------|------|------------------|------------------|
| Product management | PROD | Manage full lifecycle, adapt products based on changing needs, create retirement plans | Oversee portfolio, align objectives with business objectives, identify new opportunities |
| Market research | MRCH | Lead complex research, synthesize multiple sources, present strategic recommendations | Set research direction, develop organizational policies, communicate insights to senior leadership |
| Specialist advice | TECH | Provide advice informing operational leadership, oversee specialist advice provision | Lead and promote specialist knowledge, shape direction and high-level decisions |

### Coaching Moments

- After performance dashboard: coach on distinguishing vanity metrics from actionable insights
- After market feedback: coach on updating mental models — the market you planned for may not be the market you are in
- After strategic retrospective: coach on intellectual honesty — acknowledging failed bets is a strength
- At completion: summarize SFIA skills practiced and level-up opportunities

### Market-Driven Strategy Checkpoint

Before closing the cycle, review all decisions:
> "Were our product decisions grounded in market research and customer evidence, or did we rely on assumptions?"

If strategy drifted from market evidence, investigate the decision-making process — not just the outcomes.
